üçùPasta Bolognese

Description

This is a delicous pasta bolognese at home recipe

Ingredients

  • 2 tbsp unsalted butter
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 4 oz pancetta,finely chopped
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 1 large carrot, finely chopped
  • 1 large celery stalk, finely chopped
  • 1 lb ground beef (85% lean)
  • 1/2 lb ground pork
  • 1 cup dry white wine or red wine
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • 1 (28 oz) can crushed tomatoes
  • 3 tbsp tomato paste
  • 1/8 tsp freshly grated nutmeg
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • 1 lb fresh tagliatelle or other pasta
  • Freshly grated parmesan cheese, for serving

Steps

  1. Saut√© the pancetta: In a large, heavy-bottomed pot or Dutch oven, melt the butter with the olive oil over medium heat. Add the pancetta and cook, stirring frequently, until golden and crisp.
  2. Cook the soffritto: Add the chopped onion, carrot, and celery. Cook until the vegetables have softened and the onion is translucent, about 10 minutes.
  3. Brown the meat: Increase the heat and add the ground beef and pork. Break up the meat with a wooden spoon and cook until it is no longer pink and is well-browned. Drain off any excess fat.
  4. Deglaze with wine: Pour in the wine and cook, stirring frequently, until the liquid has evaporated, about 15 minutes.
  5. Simmer with milk: Add the milk and nutmeg. Bring to a simmer and cook until the milk has almost completely evaporated and been absorbed by the meat, about 30‚Äì45 minutes.
  6. Add tomatoes: Stir in the crushed tomatoes and tomato paste, mixing thoroughly to combine. Season with salt and pepper.
  7. Slow simmer: Bring the sauce to a gentle bubble, then reduce the heat to the lowest setting. Partially cover the pot, leaving the lid ajar. Simmer for at least 1.5 hours, but ideally for 2‚Äì3 hours, stirring occasionally. The longer it simmers, the richer the flavor will be. If the sauce becomes too dry, add a little broth or water.
  8. Cook the pasta: About 15 minutes before the sauce is finished, cook the pasta in a large pot of generously salted boiling water according to the package directions, until al dente. Reserve about 1 cup of the pasta cooking water before draining.
  9. Combine and serve: Add the drained pasta directly to the pot with the bolognese sauce. Add a splash of the reserved pasta water and toss to combine, allowing the pasta to absorb the sauce. Serve immediately, topped with plenty of grated parmesan cheese.

This is an amazing reciepe for chicken parm and pasta all in one dish!

Ingredients

  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour

  • 1 cup seasoned Italian breadcrumbs (or panko breadcrumbs)

  2. Coat and pan-fry the chicken. Dredge each chicken piece first in the flour, then dip it into the egg, and finally coat it evenly in the breadcrumb mixture.   3. Cook the pasta. While the chicken is cooking, bring a large pot of salted water to a boil and cook the pasta for about 2 minutes less than the package directions, or until al dente. This prevents the pasta from getting too soft while baking. Drain the pasta.
  4. Assemble the bake. In a large bowl, combine the drained pasta with the marinara sauce, 1/2 cup of the shredded mozzarella, and 1/4 cup of the Parmesan. Toss to combine.
  5. Layer and bake. Transfer the pasta mixture to a 9x13-inch baking dish. Arrange the breaded chicken pieces evenly over the pasta. Sprinkle the remaining mozzarella and Parmesan cheeses.
  6. Bake and broil. Cover the baking dish with foil and bake for 20‚Äì25 minutes, or until the sauce is hot and bubbly. For a crispy, golden top, remove the foil and broil for 1‚Äì2 minutes, watching carefully to prevent burning.
  7. Serve. Let the casserole rest for a few minutes before serving. Garnish with fresh basil or parsley if desired.
